Veritania.

A fictional European country. All names, numbers and events are invented for this exercise.

Country overview

Veritania is a mid-sized European country of about 8 million people. Its economy is built on farming, forestry, light manufacturing, and a growing tourism sector. Around 40% of the population lives in rural areas, and many families have farmed the same land for several generations.

Key tension
City vs. countryside

In recent years, Veritania has experienced rising costs of living, growing distrust of national institutions, and a deepening divide between urban and rural communities. Social media has become the main source of news for many people in rural areas, where local newspapers have largely disappeared.

Outdoor culture is a strong part of Veritanian identity. Hiking, hunting, and fishing are deeply embedded in rural life and have been passed down through generations. Access to open land is seen by many as a basic right, not a privilege.

The four audience groups

These are the groups your exercise asks you to target. Each has different concerns and different reasons to be sceptical of the Living Landscapes Act.

Hikers and Outdoor Enthusiasts

Walking clubs, trail runners, hunters, and anglers form a large and vocal community in Veritania. They care deeply about access to countryside, and many have used the same routes for decades. Any suggestion that land will be closed off, even in the name of conservation, raises strong objections.

Urban Residents and City Dwellers

City residents in Veritania are already dealing with rising costs of living. They may support environmental goals in principle, but opposition messaging can reach them through food prices, housing costs, and the framing that wealthy foundations are making policy decisions that ordinary people will pay for.

Young Families

Parents in Veritania are dealing with rising food prices, housing costs, and uncertainty about the future. They care about the environment but feel squeezed from every direction. Any policy that might push food prices higher or limit where their children can play outdoors is difficult to support, even if they agree with the underlying goals.

Small Business Owners

Rural tourism operators, food producers, and local suppliers are often operating on very thin margins. New licensing requirements or restrictions on land access can feel like a serious threat. Many are also sceptical that larger, better-funded organisations will benefit from the Act while smaller businesses are left to struggle.

The Living Landscapes Act

The Living Landscapes Act is a new environmental law that the Veritanian government is preparing to pass. It is supported by the ruling coalition and by most large environmental organisations. It is opposed by farming unions, rural mayors, and some opposition parties.

Requires 15% of agricultural land to be set aside for nature recovery by 2035

Introduces restricted zones along rivers and forests, limiting certain farming activities

Creates a new national authority to oversee land use and compliance

Offers subsidies for farmers who voluntarily transition land, though details are not yet confirmed

Restricts certain hunting and fishing permits in protected zones

Was backed mainly by urban environmental groups during its design phase, with limited rural consultation

The Act has genuine environmental goals. But it also has real design flaws that opposition actors can exploit. Your exercise is to find those flaws and build a message around them, so that you can understand how to counter them.
