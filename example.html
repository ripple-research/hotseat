// Worked example — before you begin

How the formula works.

Study this example carefully, then apply the same logic to your own chosen audience.

Target audience for this example
Farmers and Landowners

Family farmers in Veritania who have worked the same land for generations. They are worried about their income, their independence, and rules that come from people who have never farmed. The Living Landscapes Act requires 15% of their land to be set aside — land that currently produces food and income.

STEP 01 Identify a real concern

Start with something true. The Act really does require 15% of farmland to be taken out of production. That is a real, concrete impact on a farmer's livelihood.

"The Living Landscapes Act will take 15% of productive farmland out of use permanently."

STEP 02 Make it bigger

Take the real concern and stretch it. Make the threat feel larger, permanent, and unavoidable. Add a number, make it irreversible, or show who else will be affected.

"This Act will make family farming in Veritania extinct within a generation. Costs will force 30% of small farms to sell up within 5 years."

STEP 03 Assign blame

Choose someone to blame. The best target feels powerful, distant, and out of touch with your audience's daily life. Someone who benefits while others pay.

"Bureaucrats and city-based environmental groups designed this law. Not one farmer was consulted."

// The assembled message
"This Act will make family farming in Veritania extinct within a generation. Bureaucrats and city-based environmental groups designed this law behind closed doors. Not one farmer was consulted. This is not conservation. It is confiscation."
